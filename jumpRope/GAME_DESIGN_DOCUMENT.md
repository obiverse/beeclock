## Vision Statement

**Ntorowa** (from Ghanaian Twi, meaning "to jump/skip") brings the vibrant, communal spirit of African jump rope games to mobile devices. Two rope turners, rhythmic timing, and escalating challenges create an experience that celebrates skill, culture, and the joy of play.

## Core Mechanics

### The Double Dutch Foundation
Two virtual rope turners stand at opposite ends, turning ropes in synchronized or alternating patterns. The player controls a jumper in the center.

### Control Schemes

#### 2D Mode
- **Tap** = Jump
- **Hold** = High Jump (for double-unders)
- **Swipe Left/Right** = Dodge/Shuffle
- **Double Tap** = Spin Jump (style points)

#### 3D Mode
- **Tap** = Jump
- **Swipe Up** = High Jump
- **Swipe Down** = Duck (for high ropes)
- **Tilt** = Lateral movement
- **Two-finger tap** = Trick moves

### Rhythm System
- Ropes turn at beats-per-minute (BPM) synced to music
- Visual and audio cues signal optimal jump timing
- "Perfect", "Good", "Early", "Late" feedback
- Combo multipliers for consecutive perfect jumps

## Game Modes

### 1. Story Mode: "Village to World"
Progress through locations from local villages to international competitions:
- **Chapter 1**: Village Square (Ghana)
- **Chapter 2**: Schoolyard (Nigeria)
- **Chapter 3**: Beach Festival (Senegal)
- **Chapter 4**: Street Competition (South Africa)
- **Chapter 5**: World Championships (Global)

Each location introduces new rope patterns, music styles, and challenges.

### 2. Endless Mode: "Keep the Rhythm"
Survive as long as possible with:
- Gradually increasing BPM
- Random pattern generation
- Power-ups and obstacles
- Daily challenges for rewards

### 3. Challenge Mode
- **Speed Trials**: Reach target jumps in time limit
- **Style Masters**: Perform trick sequences
- **Endurance**: No misses for X jumps
- **Pattern Match**: Follow specific rhythm sequences

### 4. Multiplayer
- **Local**: Pass device between turns
- **Online**: Asynchronous score challenges
- **Live Battles**: Real-time rope-off competitions

## Rope Patterns (Difficulty Progression)

### Basic Patterns
1. **Single Rope** - One rope, steady rhythm
2. **Alternating** - Two ropes, alternating turns
3. **Synchronized** - Both ropes together

### Intermediate Patterns
4. **Double Dutch** - Two ropes, opposite directions
5. **Speed Burst** - Sudden tempo increase
6. **Stutter Step** - Irregular timing

### Advanced Patterns
7. **Crossover** - Ropes cross in center
8. **Wave** - Rising and falling rope heights
9. **Hurricane** - High-speed synchronized chaos

### Master Patterns
10. **The Serpent** - Unpredictable snake-like movement
11. **Ancestral Rhythm** - Complex polyrhythmic patterns
12. **Infinity Loop** - Figure-8 rope paths

## Invented Game Variants

### 1. "Shadow Jump"
- Your shadow jumps slightly behind you
- Both you AND shadow must clear the rope
- Creates a rhythmic echo effect

### 2. "Rope Weaver"
- Collect colored threads while jumping
- Weave patterns by jumping in sequences
- Complete the pattern before time runs out

### 3. "Caller's Challenge"
- One player calls out moves in real-time
- Jumper must execute the called moves
- Roles swap after each round

### 4. "Spirit Rope"
- Invisible rope that appears briefly
- Rely on audio cues and memory
- Test true rhythm mastery

### 5. "Tandem Jump"
- Control two characters jumping together
- Asymmetric timing challenges
- Cooperative scoring
